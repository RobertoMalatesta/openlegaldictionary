title: Notation
letter: N
permalink: "/definitions/notation.html"
body: In English probate prac-tice, notation is the act of making a merno-randum of
  some special circumstance on a probate or letters of administration. Thus, where
  a grant is made for the whole personal estate of the deceased within the United
  Kingdom, which can only be done in the case of a person dying domiciled in England,
  the fact of hls having been so domiciled is noted on the grant Coote, Prob. Pr.
  36; Sweet
