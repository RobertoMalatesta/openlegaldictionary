body: E’8 CASE. This case decided, in 1604, that “every man's house [meaning hls dwelling-house
  only] is his castle,” and that an officer executing civil process may not break
  open outer doors in general, but only inner doors, but that (after request made)
  he may break open even outer doors to find goods of another wrongfully in the house.
  Brown. It is reported in 5 Coke, 91
