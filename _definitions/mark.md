title: Mark
letter: M
permalink: "/definitions/mark.html"
body: '1. A character, usually in the form of a cross, made as a substitute for his
  signature by a person who cannot write, in executing a conveyance or other legal
  docu-ment. It is commonly made as follows: A third person writes the name of the
  marksman, leaving a blank space between the Christian name and surname; in this
  space the latter traces the mark, or crossed lines, and above the mark is written
  “his,” (or “her,”) and below it, “mark.”'
published_at: '2018-07-07'
source: Black's Law Dictionary
