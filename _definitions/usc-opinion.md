title: Opinion

body: A judge's written explanation of the decision of the court. Because a case may
  be heard by three or more judges in the court of appeals, the opinion in appellate
  decisions can take several forms. If all the judges completely agree on the result,
  one judge will write the opinion for all. If all the judges do not agree, the formal
  decision will be based upon the view of the majority, and one member of the majority
  will write the opinion. The judges who did not agree with the majority may write
  separately in dissenting or concurring opinions to present their views. A dissenting
  opinion disagrees with the majority opinion because of the reasoning aUS Cre ca
