---
title: Speculation
letter: S
permalink: "/definitions/speculation.html"
body: In commerce. The act or practice of buying lands, goods, etc., in expectation
  of a rise of price and of selling them at an advance, as distinguished from a regular
  trade, in which the profit expected is the difference between the retail and whole-sale
  prices, or tbe difference of price in the place where the goods are purchased, and
  the place where they are to be carried for market, webster. See Maxwell v. Burns
  (Tenn. Ch. App.) 59 S. W. 1067; U. S. v. Detroit Timber & Lumber Co. (C. C.) 124
  Fed. 393
