---
title: Right Patent
permalink: "/definitions/right-patent.html"
body: An obsolete writ, which was brought for lands and tenements, and not for an
  advowson, or common, and lay only for an estate in fee-simple, and not for him who
  had a lesser estate; as tenant in tail, tenant in frank marriage, or tenant for
  life. Fitzh. Nat. Brev. 1
published_at: '2018-07-07'
