body: In ecclesiastical law. In-duction is the ceremony by which an incum-bent who
  has been instituted to a benefice is vested with full possession of all the profits
  belonging to the church, so tiiat he becomes seised of the temporalities of the
  church, and is theu complete incumbent. It is performed by virtue of a mandate of
  in* duction directed by the bishop to the archdeacon, who either perforins it in
  person, or directs his precept to one or more other clergymen to do it Phillim.
  Ecc. Law, 477
