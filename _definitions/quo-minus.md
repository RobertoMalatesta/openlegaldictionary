---
title: Quo Minus
permalink: "/definitions/quo-minus.html"
body: Lat. A writ upon which all proceedings in the court of exchequer were formerly
  grounded. In it the plaintiff suggests that he is the king’s debtor, and that the
  defendant has done him the Injury or damage complained of, quo minus sufflcienS
  existit, by which he is less able to pay the king’s debt This was originally requisite
  in order to give Jurisdiction to the court of ex-chequer; but now this suggestion
  is a mere form. 3 Bl. Comm. 46
