title: In Initialibus
letter: I
permalink: "/definitions/in-initialibus.html"
body: 'In the prellmlna-ries. A term in Scotch practice, applied to the preliminary
  examination of a witness as to the following points: whether he knows the parties,
  or bears ill will to either of them, or has recelved any reward or promise of reward
  for what he may say, or con lose or gain by the cause, or has been told by any person
  what to say. If the witness answer these questions satisfactorily, he is then examined
  in causa, in the cause. Bell, Dict “Evidence.”'
