body: or STARRA. The old term for contract or obligation among the Jews, be-ing a
  corruption from the Hebrew word “sftefar,” a covenant By an ordinance of Richard
  I., no starr was allowed to be valid, unless deposited in one of certain repositories
  established by law, the most considerable of which was in the king's exchequer at
  westmiuster ; and Blackstone conjectures that the room in which these chests were
  kept was thence called the “starr-chamber.” 4 Bl. Comm. 266, 267, note a
