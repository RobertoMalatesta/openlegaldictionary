---
title: Mitigation
letter: M
permalink: "/definitions/mitigation.html"
body: Alleviation; abatement or diminution of a penalty or punishment Imposed by law.
  “Mitigating circumstances” are such as do not constitute a justification or excuse
  of the offense in question, but which, in fairness and mercy, may be considered
  as extenuating or reducing the degree of moral culpability. See Heaton v. wright,
  10 How. Prac. (N. Y.) 82; wandell v. Ed-wards, 25 Hun (N. Y.) 500; Hess v. New York
  Press Co., 26 App. Div. 73, 49 N. Y, Supp. 894
