The title of a work written-by St. Germain in the reign of Henry VIIL in which many principles of the common law are discussed in a popular manner. It ls in the form of a dialogue between a doctor of divinity and a student in law, and has always been considered a book of merit and authority. 1 Kent, Comm. 504; Crabb, Eng. Law, 482
