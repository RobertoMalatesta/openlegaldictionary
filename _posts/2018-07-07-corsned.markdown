In Saxon law. The morsel of execration. A species of ordeal in use among the Saxons, performed by eating a piece of bread over which the priest had pronounced a certain Imprecation. If the accused ate It freely, he was pronounced iu-nocent; but, if it stuck ln hls throat. It wns considered as a proof of his guilt. Crabb
