In ships of war, a kind of naval cadet, whose buslness ls to second or transmit the orders of the superior officers and assist in the necessary business of the vessel, but understood to be in training for a commission. A passed midshipman is 'one who has passed an examination and ls a candidate for promotion to the rank of lieu-tenant. See U. S. v. Cook, 128 U. S. 254, 9 Sup. Ct. 108, 32 L. Ed. 464
